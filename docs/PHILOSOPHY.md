# DTP Philosophy & Founding Vision

## The Problem: A Coordination Tax on Commerce

George Milton spent 13 years managing supply chain complexity at Yellowbird Foods, scaling the brand to 17,000+ retail doors. The systemic insight from that experience: the coordination overhead — spreadsheets, phone calls, faxes, paper checks, 30–90 day payment terms — is staggering relative to what actually gets accomplished.

A USDA estimate suggests middleman costs consume $375–750 billion annually in the U.S. food supply chain alone. That money is not buying better products, faster delivery, or safer food. It is paying for coordination friction that technology has made obsolete.

DTP exists to replace coordination-by-intermediary with coordination-by-protocol.

---

## Competitive Context

The problem space is not empty. Several efforts address adjacent parts of it:

- **VeChain** — supply chain traceability on-chain, primarily focused on goods tracking and post-hoc verification
- **IBM Food Trust** — enterprise traceability platform (Hyperledger), used by Walmart and others; strong on tracking, but closed infrastructure requiring IBM participation
- **OpenAI Agentic Commerce Protocol** — emerging agent-to-agent commerce primitives, oriented toward B2C and digital goods

The underserved gap is **B2B wholesale coordination**: the negotiation, contracting, settlement, and traceability layer between producers and buyers of physical goods. Existing platforms either focus on post-hoc tracking or consumer-facing marketplaces. None provide open infrastructure for the full wholesale trade lifecycle — from intent to settlement to regulatory compliance — without extracting rent from every transaction.

DTP targets that gap.

## The Ten Commandments of Modern Commerce

The philosophical foundation of DTP is captured in ten principles for how commerce *should* work when technology makes extractive intermediation unnecessary.

Published March 2026 in [*Gross To Net*](https://georgemilton.substack.com).

---

### I. Information Is Not a Commodity to Be Gatekept

Information should not generate profit through access control alone. Curation and synthesis provide genuine value; controlling access to existing information does not.

*In DTP: trade data, reputation scores, and regulatory credentials live on-chain and are readable by any conforming implementation. No platform can gatekeep a participant's business identity.*

---

### II. Value Equals Transformation

Genuine value creation requires substantive change — discovery, creation, improvement, movement, teaching, or informed curation. Mere intermediation between willing parties is extraction, not value creation.

*In DTP: the protocol charges no rent. Brokers who add no transformation are not required steps in the trade flow.*

---

### III. Original Work Deserves Protection

Creators who reveal something genuinely new through research, craft, or risk deserve compensation. This is the only justified competitive advantage.

*In DTP: certifications, quality grades, and credentials carry issuer, expiry, and verification source. Nothing is self-reported without attestation. Honest differentiation is surfaced and permanent; false differentiation cannot persist.*

---

### IV. Breakthroughs Belong to Everyone (Eventually)

Innovation cycles through creation, earned reward, and broad access. The system fails when intermediaries capture value meant for creators or the public.

*In DTP: the protocol is open-source and non-extractive. There is no speculative token. Any fees are set by governance and flow to infrastructure — not captured by a private platform.*

---

### V. Commerce Serves Society

Commerce exists to serve human needs. The relationship inverts into dysfunction when society is made to serve commerce's extraction requirements.

*In DTP: FSMA 204 compliance is built into the core protocol, not bolted on as an add-on fee. Food safety is a societal good, not a product.*

---

### VI. Customers Benefit First

Markets work when customers benefit most, followed by organizations that genuinely serve them. Capital gains are secondary consequences, never primary objectives.

*In DTP: the two-sided matching model ensures buyers and sellers find each other directly. Neither is forced to pay platform rent to access the other.*

---

### VII. Quality Should Determine Price

Quality obscurity allows middlemen to thrive while producers suffer. Transparent quality signals allow markets to price accurately.

*In DTP: on-chain reputation, verifiable certifications, and COA document anchoring make quality claims auditable and permanent. Buyers can verify; sellers can prove.*

---

### VIII. Efficiency Gains Must Be Shared

When supply chains become faster or cheaper, those improvements should distribute throughout the system — not accumulate at chokepoints.

*In DTP: financed payment terms target 30% effective APR versus the legacy distributor benchmark of ~67.6% APR implied by common 2/10 net 30 terms. Smart contract escrow efficiency translates to lower cost of capital for buyers and faster settlement for sellers.*

---

### IX. Dignified Living Is Not a Profit Center

All participants deserve affordable access to dignified living. Baseline survival costs should not function as margin extraction opportunities.

*In DTP: transparent pricing, portable reputation, and direct trade capability reduce the structural disadvantage small producers face against large aggregators and incumbent distributors.*

---

### X. Direct Transaction Is a Right

People require direct transaction capability without intermediary permission. Trust should be earned, verifiable, and participant-controlled — never platform-dependent.

*In DTP: every party's reputation, certifications, and trade history live in the NEAR contract, not in any platform's database. Accounts are never locked to a platform. Trust is portable by design.*

---

## Building in Public

DTP is pre-alpha public infrastructure — not a startup seeking to capture the value it displaces. The code is open. The spec is open. The goal is a protocol layer that makes the extractive middleman structurally unnecessary, the same way email made the postal telegraph structurally unnecessary for short messages.

The ten commandments are not marketing copy. They are the design constraints.
